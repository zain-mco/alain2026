WELCOME MESSAGE

FROM THE
CONFERENCE CHAIRMAN

It is with great enthusiasm that I welcome you to the 3rd Al Ain Neurology Conference 2026, to be held this January in the culturally rich and serene city of Al Ain, UAE.

This conference serves as a platform for knowledge exchange, innovation, and collaboration among the brightest minds in neurology. Our goal is to create an environment that not only highlights the latest advancements in neurological science but also encourages meaningful dialogue that can shape future directions in research and clinical practice.

The program will feature esteemed speakers, cutting-edge scientific content, and hands-on workshops tailored to address current challenges and opportunities in the field. Whether you're a seasoned expert or an emerging professional, we believe you'll find immense value in the discussions, connections, and shared experiences.

We look forward to welcoming you to what promises to be an inspiring and impactful event.

Thank you

OBJECTIVES

3rd Al Ain Neurology Conference

01

KNOWLEDGE EXCHANGE

Facilitate the sharing of cutting-edge research findings, clinical innovations, and best practices in neurology among healthcare professionals, researchers, and academics from diverse backgrounds.

02

PROMOTION OF COLLABORATION

Encourage interdisciplinary collaboration among neurologists, neuroscientists, clinicians, and industry stakeholders to accelerate the translation of research into practical advancements in neurological care.

03

EDUCATION AND TRAINING

Provide high-quality educational sessions, workshops, and training programs aimed at enhancing the clinical skills and scientific knowledge of attendees at all levels of their careers.

04

HIGHLIGHTING EMERGING TRENDS

Showcase the latest trends and technological advancements in neurology, including developments in neuroimaging, neurogenetics, neurorehabilitation, neuroethics, and digital health solutions.

05

NETWORKING AND COMMUNITY BUILDING

Create meaningful networking opportunities to connect participants, foster professional relationships, and strengthen a collaborative and inclusive neurology community within the region and beyond.
